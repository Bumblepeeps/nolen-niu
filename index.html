XO

The XO™ sofa is a 2-seater concept blending chaise lounge style seating orientation within a traditional 3-seater format. Large armrest surfaces and faceted sides create comfortable conversational seating. It can also be placed back to back creating a dynamic commercial seating environment. Commercial grade High Resilience foam along with a Poplar hardwood frame ensures longevity.

ZERO

The ZERO™ chaise lounge is individually hand crafted in the U.S. and made-to-order with your choice of material colors and finishes. The 1” thick base is composed of stainless steel and the 2” seat profile is constructed with tubular steel and lined with commercial grade High Resilience foam for longevity. At its seat, the ZERO™ has a maximum weight capacity of 400 pounds and an impressive 200 pounds weight limit at the footrest.

EVO

The EVO™ sofa is a modular seating concept with components that mix and match to create dynamic seating environments. Its deep recesses are the result of creating three individual seating zones enabling support and creating comfort for the seat, lumbar, and back. They can also be used for magazine storage or remote controls. Cleaning is simplified with the use of the narrow nozzle vacuum attachment.

LOX

The LOX™ sofa is all about comfort. Big, plush, comfy seating that is retro in styling and contained in a modern day tightly fitted leather body. With its wrap around shell, pillows can be easily accommodated while the shallow seating itself is designed to support the lower back. Stitching on the leather corresponds to the fabric, which is offered in a large variety of vivid colors.

DIVIDE

The DIVIDE™ sofa is a balance between form and function. The separation between the backrest and seat is to eliminate fabric tension typical of one-piece sofas. This allows the seat to perform as an independent cushion for added comfort. The gap also eliminates the need of flipping up seat cushions revealing mystery items. Loose change is in plain view and cleaning is simplified with the use of a narrow nozzle vacuum attachment.

YO

The YO™ armchair focuses on seating posture and comfort. Soft contours of the backrest and shallow seat depth both contribute to the armchair’s surprising comfort. It uses firm High Resilience foams along the backrest with a slightly softer seat cushion to provide the lower back support similar to most task chairs. It also allows you to sit taller with your lower back firmly seated in place.

MIRROR

The MIRROR™ bed and nightstands complements the existing sectional concept with a headboard that has two independent flip up headrests. This innovative bed system assembles with no hardware and simply clips together with latches in four separate parts: the headboard, rails, and footboard. The wood sides are offered in a variety of veneers including Macassar, Mahogany, Maple, Rift White Oak, Walnut, and Wenge. The nightstands have satin painted MDF shelves which can be custom colored to match any fabric or leather selection for the headboard and footboard. Another unique feature of the MIRROR™ bed is the upholstered footboard.

TOPIC

The TOPIC™ barstool was value engineered with its four legs using the same bent solid steel rod that are welded together at its center axis. The barstool itself consists of two parts which are the rolled steel seat and bent solid steel rod legs. These pieces are mass-produced then welded together and powder coated, requiring no assembly. It’s offered in a wide range of powder coated RAL colors.

HORIZON





The HORIZON™ dining table has an uncompromising tabletop that looks wet to the touch yet durable enough to resist most scratches and abrasions. The dining table has a weighted pedestal base that keeps it balanced on each end while still light enough to handle thanks to the Ultra-Light MDF core beneath the tough laminate surface. Adjustable glides ensure an even dining experience and the tabletop wipes clean with any glass cleaner and a soft cloth. The edge of the tabletop also tapers to the base, increasing leg space underneath.

CLOAK

The CLOAK™ sofa combines low back sofa aesthetics with high back comfort. The seat and back dive deep between the tapered upholstered arms which aid in easy entry and exiting. The plush goose down wrapped over the High Resilience foam creates support for the low back without compromising the softness in feel. The modular ottoman fits snug in front of the sofa adding extra support to the legs while completing the chaise lounge sit and feel.

POLY

The POLY™ sofa was designed to utilize just-in-time manufacturing where the simplification of the fabrication process and minimizing material wastes allows faster lead times with more competitive pricing. Its 2D graphic inspired design embraces the elegance of basic geometry and its shallow seat depth offers more support to the lower back, improving overall posture and comfort.

bio

Nolen Niu is an American Industrial Designer recognized for his holistic approach to design and clean, provocative aesthetics. After receiving his Bachelors of Science in Industrial Design from the world-renowned Art Center College of Design, in Pasadena, California, Niu developed private furniture lines, custom one-of-a-kind pieces, mass production furniture, residential and retail spaces for corporate, private and celebrity clientele.

In 2005, Niu ventured out globally and began developing a collection of upholstered goods under the Nolen Niu brand. He was one of only a few American designers to achieve selection and awarded the opportunity to exhibit at the 2006 Salone Satellite Milan Furniture Fair where he debuted his first collection, which received enthusiastic reviews from the international community. In conjunction with his selection for the Salone Satellite, he was one of ten designers chosen to be showcased at the ICFF Studio / Bernhardt Design joint venture exhibit, a juried event aimed to support emerging talent. Niu has also completed the sixth rendition of the Annual Esquire Magazine House, where he was one of four designers chosen to design a room in a 7,000 square foot ultra modern mansion, located high atop Hollywood Hills, which hosted celebrity driven charity events for multiple organizations.

In 2011, he opened his own production facility, specializing in upholstery and carpentry, located in the heart of downtown Los Angeles. This makes him one of the few American furniture designers who are completely vertically integrated, with the ability to competitively design and manufacture his products all in America.

Born and raised in the Washington D.C. metro area in 1975, Niu moved to Los Angeles, California in 1996 to attend Art Center College of Design, Pasadena where he is also an active faculty member today. Niu’s corporate office is located in Beverly Hills with a private studio and production facility in downtown Los Angeles.
